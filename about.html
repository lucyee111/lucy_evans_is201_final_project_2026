My Story

Marketing, Data, and a Grain of Film

I'm a Junior at Brigham Young University studying Marketing, with an emphasis on data analytics and consumer behavior. I'm drawn to the intersection of creative storytelling and measurable strategy — the idea that a great campaign should be both beautiful and effective.

Outside the classroom, I've developed an obsession with film photography. There's something deeply satisfying about working with a medium that demands patience: choosing your frame carefully, trusting the exposure, and waiting to see what you got back from the lab. It's the opposite of the instant-gratification scroll, and I love it for exactly that reason.

I started shooting 35mm a couple of years ago with a hand-me-down camera and a roll of Kodak Gold 200. I've since expanded into medium format and am slowly building a collection of film stocks I love. My favorites are Portra 400 for portraits and Ilford HP5 for anything black and white.

I grew up in Highland, Utah — so coming to BYU felt like home, but in the best way. Highland is the kind of place where you grow up hiking the mountains in your backyard, which probably has something to do with why I'm so drawn to photography.
